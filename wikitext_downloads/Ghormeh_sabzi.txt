'''Ghormeh sabzi''' ({{langx|fa|قورمه‌ سبزی}}), also known as '''[[khoresh]]t sabzi''' ({{lang|fa|خورشت سبزی}}), also spelled ''qormeh sabzi'', is an [[Iranian cuisine|Iranian]] [[herb]] [[stew]]. It is considered the [[national dish]] and is a very commonly served dish in [[Iran]].<ref>{{cite book|last1=Dana-Haeri|first1=Jila|last2=Ghorashian|first2=Shahrzad|last3=Lowe|first3=Jason|title=New Persian Cooking: A Fresh Approach to the Classic Cuisine of Iran|date=2011|publisher=I. B. Tauris|isbn=978-1848855861 |page=79}}</ref> ''Ghormeh sabzi'' has different variants, which are based on the difference between beans and meat.

==Preparation==
[[File:Ailin14VeganGhormehSabzi.JPG|thumb|Homemade ''ghormeh sabzi'' served with [[Pilaf|chelow]] and [[Shirazi salad]]]]

The main ingredients are a mixture of [[Sautéing|sauteed]] herbs, consisting mainly of [[parsley]], [[leek]]s or [[Scallion|green onions]], and [[coriander]], seasoned with the key spice of dried [[fenugreek]] leaves. The herb mixture has many variations.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Persian Stew Recipe Iranian Ghormeh Sabzi International Cuisines | website=[[YouTube]] | date=14 September 2020 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UOa-rm-4ulA|language=ja-JP |access-date=2022-06-14}}</ref>
Any dark bitter greens can be used, such as [[kale]], [[Brassica juncea|mustard greens]], or [[turnip greens]], although none of those are part of the original recipe.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.unicornsinthekitchen.com/ghormeh-sabzi-persian-herb-stew/ |title=Ghormeh Sabzi - Persian Herb Stew (Video) |first=Shadi|last=HasanzadeNemati|date=January 3, 2018 |publisher=Unicorns in the Kitchen}}</ref>

This mixture is cooked with [[kidney beans]], yellow or [[red onions]], [[black lime]] (pierced dried limou-Amani [[Persian lime]]), and [[turmeric]]-seasoned lamb or beef. The dish is then served with polo (Persian parboiled and steamed [[rice]]) or over [[tahdig]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.196flavors.com/iran-ghormeh-sabzi/|title=Ghormeh Sabzi|first=Mike|last=Benayoun|date=December 11, 2022}}</ref> 

The ''Financial Times'' noted that there is much disagreement among Iranians on the ingredients used in the stew.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Patalay |first=Ajesh |date=2020-09-03|title=Where to eat Persian food – just as mother makes it |url=https://www.ft.com/content/07343b2e-3b85-45ab-a722-8d23fb31f0a4|access-date=2021-01-22 |website=Financial Times |language=en-GB}}</ref>

== Serving==
''Ghormeh sabzi'', a flavorful stew, is traditionally served with [[Basmati|basmati rice]]. However, it can also be eaten with flatbread, [[Shirazi salad]] (cucumbers, tomatoes, onions, and herbs), and [[Tzatziki|mast-o-khiar]], a yogurt-cucumber dip.<ref>{{cite web |title=Explore Persian Ghormeh Sabzi: A Flavorful Herb Stew from Iran's Rich Culinary Heritage |url=https://cookingcounty.top/persian-ghormeh-sabzi/ |publisher=Cooking County |access-date=7 April 2023}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Marzia |date=2023-02-16 |title=Ghormeh Sabzi Recipe (Persian Herb Stew) |url=https://littlespicejar.com/ghormeh-sabzi/ |access-date=2024-04-01 |website=Little Spice Jar |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Mast o Khiar Persian Yogurt and Cucumber Dip|publisher=Unicorns in the Kitchen|author =HasanzadeNemati, Shadi|date=2021-07-08|url=https://www.unicornsinthekitchen.com/mast-o-khiar/|access-date=2025-02-11}}</ref>

== Cultural importance ==
[[Self (magazine)|''Self'' magazine]] listed ''ghormeh sabzi'' as one of twelve of the most meaningful dishes among cultures passed down among families.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Shiffer|first=Emily|title=12 People on the Meaningful Food Traditions Passed Down in Their Families|url=https://www.self.com/story/food-traditions|access-date=2021-01-22|website=SELF|date=20 January 2021 |language=en-us}}</ref>  The ''[[Tehran Times]]'' wrote that the dish "is one of the most prominent dishes in Persian culinary heritage."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-14|title=Persian cuisine: Khoresh-e Ghormeh Sabzi (Persian herb, bean and lamb stew)|url=https://www.tehrantimes.com/news/451223/Persian-cuisine-Khoresh-e-Ghormeh-Sabzi-Persian-herb-bean|access-date=2021-01-22|website=Tehran Times|language=en}}</ref> 

Iranians in the diaspora traditionally celebrate "International Ghormeh Sabzi Appreciation Day" two days after [[Thanksgiving]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=KUMS|title=Ghorme Sabzi|url=https://www.kums.ac.ir/en/students/is/dining/iiris/ghormesabzi|access-date=2021-01-22|website=KUMS|language=en}}</ref>
