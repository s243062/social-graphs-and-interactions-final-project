'''Idrijski žlikrofi''' are traditional [[Slovenian cuisine|Slovenian]] dumplings that originate from [[Idrija]].<ref>{{cite book|title=Strategija razvoja gastronomije Slovenije|year=2006|publisher=Univerza v Mariboru|location=Maribor}}</ref> They are made from dough with potato filling and are often served either as a side dish to meat or on their own, in which case they are topped with breadcrumbs.<ref name="Adamlje1995">{{cite book|last=Adamlje|first=Slavko|title=Slovenska kuhinja|year=1995|publisher=Mladinska knjiga|location=Ljubljana|page=123}}</ref> The recipe dates back to the mid 19th century and remains one of the most popular Slovenian dishes.<ref>{{cite web|last=Prešeren|first=Polona|title=Idrijski žlikrofi - a true speciality of Idrija|url=http://www.slovenia.si/visit/cuisine/modern-culinary-trends/idrijski-zlikrofi-a-true-speciality-of-idrija/|access-date=2013-06-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180506084149/http://www.slovenia.si/visit/cuisine/modern-culinary-trends/idrijski-zlikrofi-a-true-speciality-of-idrija|archive-date=2018-05-06|url-status=dead}}</ref> Žlikrofi were awarded an EU [[protected geographical status]] in 2010 as [[Traditional Speciality Guaranteed]], the first Slovenian dish to do so.<ref>{{cite news|title=Idrijski žlikrofi tudi evropsko zaščiteni|url=http://www.rtvslo.si/gospodarstvo/idrijski-zlikrofi-tudi-evropsko-zasciteni/221382|accessdate=23 June 2013|date=15 January 2010}}</ref>
