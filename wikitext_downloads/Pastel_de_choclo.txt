'''Pastel de choclo''' ("corn [[pie]]" or "corn [[cake]]") is a [[South American cuisine|South American]] dish made from [[sweetcorn]] or [[choclo]]. It is similar to the pastel de elote found in [[Mexican cuisine]] and to the English [[corn pudding]]. The filling usually contains [[ground beef]], [[Chicken (food)|chicken]], [[raisin]]s, [[black olive]]s, [[onion]]s, or slices of [[hard boiled egg]].<ref>Albala Ken. 'Food Cultures of the World.' ABC-CLIO Vol. I, pp. 69-70.</ref> It is traditional in the cuisines of Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Paraguay, Peru, and Uruguay.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Weston |first=Rosario Olivas |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=mgwKHAAACAAJ |title=Cultura identidad y cocina en el Perú |date=1993 |publisher=Escuela Profesional de turismo y Hotelería, Facultad de Ciencias de la Comunicación, Turismo y Sicología, Universidad San Martin de Porres |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=PERU21 |first=NOTICIAS |date=2017-05-06 |title=Tacna: Del picante a la tacneña al pastel de choclo {{!}} CULTURA |url=https://peru21.pe/cultura/tacna-picante-tacnena-pastel-choclo-75287-noticia/ |access-date=2023-08-08 |website=Peru21 |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=admin |title=¡Una receta super fácil! / El pastel de choclo, una comida típica salteña que disfruta toda la familia |url=https://www.saltasoy.com.ar/una-receta-super-facil-el-pastel-de-choclo-una-comida-tipica-saltena-que-disfruta-toda-la-familia/ |access-date=2023-08-08 |website=www.saltasoy.com.ar}}</ref>

==Preparation==
Pastel de choclo is prepared with sweetcorn ground into a paste. It is then seasoned with ground [[Ocimum basilicum|basil]] that is blended into the corn. The mixture is pre-cooked with milk and a little [[lard]] and used as a topping for the filling. The filling, known as "''pino''", contains minced beef cooked with onions, [[paprika]], other spices, and sometimes chicken, is also used as a filling for traditional Chilean [[empanada]]s.<ref>[http://southamericanfood.about.com/od/snacksstreetfood/r/empanadaschile.htm Chilean Empanadas - Empanadas de Pino] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130326110901/http://southamericanfood.about.com/od/snacksstreetfood/r/empanadaschile.htm |date=2013-03-26 }} aboutchile.com Marian Blazes Retrieved March 17, 2013</ref> The ''pino'' is laid in the bottom of the ''paila'' with slices of hard boiled egg, olives and raisins.

==In popular culture==
The Argentinian poet Florencio Escardó wrote the following ode to pastel de choclo, published in 1876:<ref>Escardó, Florencio "Reseña histórica, estadística y descriptiva con tradiciones orales de las Republicas Argentina y Oriental del Uruguay." La Tribuna, Montevideo, 1876. p. 129.</ref>

Y díganme con franqueza, ¿hay cosa mas deliciosa que un pastel de choclo?
