'''''Moules-frites''''' or '''''moules et frites'''''{{sfn|Spoiden|2001|p=162}} ({{IPA|fr|mul.fʁit}}]; {{langx|nl|mosselen-friet}}) is a main dish of [[Mussel#As food|mussels]] and [[French fries]] originating in Northern France and Belgium.{{sfn|Malgieri|2011}} The name is French, ''moules'' meaning mussels and ''frites'' fries, with the Dutch name for the dish meaning the same. It is the [[national dish]] of Belgium.{{sfn|Spoiden|2001|p=169}}

==Background==
Although ''moules-frites'' are popular in many countries, it is thought that the dish originated in Belgium.{{sfn|''ifood.tv''}} It is likely that it was originally created by combining [[Mussel#As food|mussels]], a popular and cheap foodstuff eaten around the [[Flanders|Flemish]] coast, and fried potatoes, which were commonly eaten around the country in winter when no fish or other food was available.{{sfn|''ifood.tv''}}

In both Belgium and France, ''moules-frites'' are available in most restaurants, depending on season. According to a survey conducted by [[Taylor Nelson Sofres|TNS]], ''moules-frites'' was identified as the second-favourite dish in France, receiving a vote of 20 per cent, narrowly losing to ''[[:fr:magret|magret de canard]]'' which received 21 per cent.{{sfn|''Les News Nutrition''|2011}}

On average, between 25 and 30 tonnes of mussels are consumed each year in Belgium as ''moules-frites''.{{sfn|Coenart|2011}}{{dubious|Only 25 to 30 tonnes of moules?|date=January 2020}} Much of the mussels consumed in Belgium come from mussel farms in nearby [[Zeeland]] in the Netherlands.{{sfn|Spoiden|2001|p=168}}

==Variants and preparation==
===''Moules''===
[[File:AuPreSale Moules.JPG|thumb|''Moules marinière'' in Belgium]]
The ways in which the mussels are cooked in the dish can vary significantly. Some common variants include:
*''Moules marinière'': Probably the most common and internationally recognisable recipe,{{sfn|Milano|2016}} ''moules marinière'' includes [[white wine]], [[shallot]]s, [[parsley]], and butter.{{sfn|Thompson}}
*''Moules nature'': The mussels are steamed with celery, leeks, and butter.{{sfn|Malgieri|2011}}
*''Moules à la crème'': Another common recipe, thickened with flour and cream.{{sfn|Malgieri|2011}}
*''Moules parquées'': A dish, probably originating in [[Brussels]], of raw mussels on the half-shell, served with a [[lemon]]-[[Mustard (condiment)|mustard]] sauce. 
*''Moules à la bière'': Mussels cooked in a sauce containing [[Belgian beer|beer]] instead of white wine.{{sfn|''Where the Food Is''|2012}}
*''Moules à l'ail'': Mussels cooked with sliced or minced [[garlic]].{{sfn|Malgieri|2011}}

Less commonly, [[Fusion cuisine|fusion variants]] are seen in which the stock may be flavoured with non-local ingredients such as [[Espelette pepper]] or [[Pernod Ricard|Pernod liquor]].{{sfn|Malgieri|2011}} They can also be served with "''Mosselsaus''", a sauce that is made with mayonnaise, mustard, and vinegar.

===''Frites''===
In various forms, ''frites'' or ''friet'' play an important role in Belgian culture and cuisine. Within Belgium, [[bintje]] potatoes are generally preferred as a basis to make fries because of their high [[starch]] content.{{sfn|Spoiden|2001|p=166}}{{sfn|Malgieri|2011}} They are generally double-fried (fried, left to cool and then fried again) in order to make them both moist in the core and crisp on the outside.{{sfn|Spoiden|2001|p=166}}

===Presentation===
As a dish, the ''moules'' and the ''frites'' are usually served separately, to avoid the fries becoming soggy in the sauce. Often, the ''moules'' are served in the pan used to cook them.{{sfn|Spoiden|2001|p=159}} A second dish is generally provided for the discarded mussel shells.{{Cn|date=April 2025}}

== Consumption ==
Mussels and French fries are served everywhere in Belgium, from the Belgian coast to the bottom of the Ardennes. In France, the dish is common in the Nord-Pas-de-Calais. It is the typical dish of the [[Braderie de Lille|braderie of Lille]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Braderie de Lille 2017 : qu'est-ce qu'on mange ? Des moules-frites !|url=https://france3-regions.francetvinfo.fr/hauts-de-france/nord-0/lille/braderie-lille-2017-qu-est-ce-qu-on-mange-moules-frites-1320435.html|website=france3-regions.francetvinfo.fr|year=2017|access-date=10 April 2019}}.</ref> In 2009, 500 tons of mussels and 30 tons of fries were consumed during this event.<ref>{{cite web|title=Braderie de Lille : 2 millions de visiteurs attendus ce week-end|url=http://www.elle.fr/elle/Loisirs/Sorties/News/Braderie-de-Lille-2-millions-de-visiteurs-attendus-ce-week-end/%28gid%29/1328876|author=Axelle Szczygiel|agency=Elle|year=2010|access-date=10 April 2019}}.</ref> During this weekend, restaurant owners pile up the mussels eaten in front of their establishments; most of the time, the restaurant Aux moules (rue de Béthune) and La Chicorée (place Rihour) have the largest quantities.<ref>{{cite web|title=Braderie de Lille : moules-frites, les coulisses|url=https://france3-regions.francetvinfo.fr/hauts-de-france/2013/08/31/braderie-de-lille-moules-frites-les-coulisses-310203.html|author=EP|agency=France 3 Nord-Pas-de-Calais|year=2013|access-date=10 April 2019}}.</ref>   

In 2008, TNS Sofres carried out a survey among the people of the North. Mussels and French fries came in second place with 25%. The dish is outstripped by [[steak frites]] which gets 33%.<ref>{{cite web|title=Les plats préférés des Français|url=https://www.tns-sofres.com/publications/les-plats-preferes-des-francais-2011|agency=TNS Sofres|year=2011|access-date=10 April 2019|archive-date=10 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190410120915/https://www.tns-sofres.com/publications/les-plats-preferes-des-francais-2011|url-status=dead}}.</ref> In France, according to the same TNS-Sofres poll, moules-frites are in second place among the favorite dishes of the French with 20%.<ref>{{cite web|title=Le magret de canard, plat préféré des Français, devant les moules-frites|url=http://news.doctissimo.fr/Nutrition/Le-magret-de-canard-plat-prefere-des-Francais-devant-les-moules-frites-25561|agency=Doctissimo|year=2011|access-date=10 April 2019|archive-date=3 January 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120103113349/http://news.doctissimo.fr/Nutrition/Le-magret-de-canard-plat-prefere-des-Francais-devant-les-moules-frites-25561|url-status=dead}}.</ref> It is one point behind duck breast.      

The dish can be consumed with an abbey lager<ref name="Willem">{{cite book|access-date=17 October 2015|date=1998|first1=Louis|isbn=2-8046-0229-X|last1=Willem|oclc=907015636|page=20|title=La Cuisine bruxelloise: traditions et créations au fil des saisons|chapter=Un complet, un ! Les moules-frites|publisher=Renaissance Du Livre |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=8y5dO7k1KFkC}}.</ref> or a dry white wine;<ref name="Willem"/> red wine should be avoided because of its tannins.<ref>{{cite news|title=Quels vins boire avec des moules frites ?|url=https://www.toutlevin.com/article/quels-vins-boire-avec-des-moules-frites|author=Alexandra Reveillon|website=toutlevin.com|access-date=10 April 2019|publication-date=}}.</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Accord mets/vins : que boire avec… des moules-frites ?|url=https://avis-vin.lefigaro.fr/connaitre-deguster/o31855-accord-mets-vins-que-boire-avec-des-moules-frites|author=Enrico Bernardo|website=lefigaro.fr|date=4 June 2015|access-date=10 April 2019}}.</ref> As for sauces, in addition to the marinière sauce that already accompanies the mussels, it is possible to add mayonnaise with the fries.<ref name="peau VdN">{{cite web|title=Qui peut faire la peau à la "moules-frites" ?|url=http://www.lavoixdunord.fr/Braderie-de-Lille/La-Braderie/Actualite/2010/09/05/article_qui-peut-faire-la-peau-a-la-moules-frite.shtml|agency=La Voix du Nord|year=2010|access-date=9 April 2019}}.</ref> Mussels and fries are also the specialty of the Belgian restaurant chain Chez Léon.<ref>{{cite web|title=Des moules et des sondages chez Léon|url=http://www.lefigaro.fr/emploi/2007/01/15/01010-20070115ARTWWW90494-des_moules_et_des_sondages_chez_leon.php|author=Jean-Bernard Litzler|website=lefigaro.fr|year=2007|access-date=10 April 2019}}.</ref>
