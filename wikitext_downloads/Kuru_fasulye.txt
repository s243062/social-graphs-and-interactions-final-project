'''Kuru fasulye''' is a [[stewed]] [[bean]] dish in [[Turkish cuisine]].<ref>From the editor Afar May 2014 page 18</ref><ref>[http://www.deliciousistanbul.com/blog/2014/02/02/white-bean-stew-kuru-fasulye/ White bean stew] Delicious Istanbul</ref> It is made primarily with [[common bean|white beans]] and [[olive oil]], and [[onion]] and [[tomato paste]] or [[tomato sauce]] are almost invariably used. Sometimes other vegetables or meat may also be added, especially [[pastirma]]. Kuru fasulye is often served along with [[cacık]] and [[rice]] or [[bulgur]]. It is often considered the [[national dish]] of Turkey.<ref>[http://turkishfood.about.com/od/BeansRiceGrains/r/Navy-Bean-Stew-Considered-Turkey-S-National-Dish.htm Navy Bean Stew And Rice Is Turkey's National Dish] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181226023134/https://www.thespruceeats.com/turkish-style-pinto-beans-3274130 |date=2018-12-26 }} turkishfood.about.com</ref><ref>[http://www.sabah.com.tr/yazarlar/pazar/ors/2012/12/16/kuru-fasulyemi-geri-istiyorum Kuru fasulyemi geri istiyorum!] Sabah</ref>{{not in source|date=November 2025}}

Also known as Mash Shorbasi in Azerbaijan.
