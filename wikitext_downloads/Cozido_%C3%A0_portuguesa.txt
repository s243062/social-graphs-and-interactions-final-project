==Preparation and ingredients==
''Cozido à Portuguesa'' is prepared with a multitude of vegetables ([[Brassica oleracea|cabbage]]s, [[Phaseolus vulgaris|bean]]s, [[potato]]es, [[carrot]]s, [[turnip]]s, [[rice]]), meat ([[chicken as food|chicken]], [[pork ribs]], [[bacon]], pork [[pig's ear (food)|ear]] and [[pig's trotters|trotters]], various parts of [[beef]]), and [[smoked sausage]]s ([[chouriço]], [[farinheira]], [[morcela]]), among others. In the [[São Miguel Island]], [[Azores]], a local version of the Cozido à Portuguesa is cooked underground with heat and steam coming from the volcanic phenomena in the area.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://en.azoresguide.net/how-to-make-and-where-to-eat-cozido-das-furnas/|title=How to make and where to eat Cozido das Furnas?|website=Açores Natureza Viva|publisher=Via Oceânica|access-date=30 August 2022|date=11 May 2021}}</ref>
