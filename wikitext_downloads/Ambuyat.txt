'''Ambuyat''' is a dish derived from the interior trunk of the [[sago]] palm. It is a starchy, bland substance similar to [[tapioca]]. Ambuyat is the [[National dish#By country|national dish]] of [[Brunei]],<ref name="Ambuyat iconic heritage">{{cite news|url=http://www.bt.com.bn/art-culture/2011/01/08/ambuyat-our-iconic-heritage|title=Ambuyat – Our iconic heritage|last=Tiah|first=Jessica|work=The Brunei Times|date=8 January 2011|access-date=5 April 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140404182236/http://www.bt.com.bn/art-culture/2011/01/08/ambuyat-our-iconic-heritage|archive-date=4 April 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://mediapermata.com.bn/ambuyat-sajian-pilihan-orang-kitani/|title=Ambuyat sajian pilihan orang kitani|trans-title=Ambuyat is the favourite dish of our people|last=Zainuddin|first=Afiq|language=ms|work=Media Permata|date=5 December 2023|access-date=10 April 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250410035502/https://mediapermata.com.bn/ambuyat-sajian-pilihan-orang-kitani/|archive-date=10 April 2025|url-status=dead}}</ref> and it is also popular in the Malaysian states of [[Sarawak]] and [[Sabah]] as well as the federal territory of [[Labuan]], where it is sometimes known as ''linut''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://munchmalaysia.com/traditional-food/ambuyat-the-traditional-and-exotic-food-gem/|title=Ambuyat: The Traditional and Exotic Food Gem|last=Jayaweera|first=Himavee|work=Munch Malaysia|date=16 October 2023 |access-date=10 April 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250410022300/https://munchmalaysia.com/traditional-food/ambuyat-the-traditional-and-exotic-food-gem/|archive-date=10 April 2025|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Background==
Ambuyat is the traditional food of the [[Bruneian Malay people|Bruneian Malay]], [[Bisaya (Borneo)|Bisaya]], [[Bajau]], [[Kadazan-Dusun]], and [[Lun Bawang|Lundayeh/Lun Bawang]] people in both the [[Interior Division]] and [[West Coast Division]] of Sabah and the [[Limbang Division]] of Sarawak, as well as the Malaysian federal territory of [[Labuan]] and the sultanate of [[Brunei]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/bhplus-old/2016/10/198020/ambuyat-sajian-tradisional-masyarakat-borneo|title=Ambuyat sajian tradisional masyarakat Borneo|trans-title=Ambuyat, a traditional dish of Borneo people|last=Lisut|first=Osman|language=ms|work=Berita Harian|date=2 October 2016|access-date=10 April 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250410023203/https://www.bharian.com.my/bhplus-old/2016/10/198020/ambuyat-sajian-tradisional-masyarakat-borneo|archive-date=10 April 2025|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="Ambuyat dipped">{{cite web|url=https://ecentral.my/makanan-sabah/|title=10 Makanan Sabah Yang Unik dan Popular di Malaysia|trans-title=10 Unique and Popular Sabah Foods in Malaysia|author=Mel|language=ms|work=eCentral|date=19 March 2024|access-date=10 April considered the [[National dish#By country|national dish]].<ref name="Ambuyat iconic heritage"/><ref>{{cite Ambuyat is eaten with a linked pair of bamboo [[chopsticks]] called ''chandas'', by rolling the starch around the prongs and then dipping it into a [[Sambal|sauce]],<ref name="Ambuyat dipped"/> of which there are many varieties, including [[shrimp paste]] (''sambal belacan''), ''sambal [[tempoyak]]'' (chilli and fermented [[durian]] paste), ''sambal [[cincalok]]'' (chilli and fermented shrimp paste), and ''sambal binjai'' (a type of [[Mangifera indica|wild mango]]).<ref name="Asian Foodies AirAsia">{{cite news|url=https://explore.airasia.com/assets/bltb477c7bb4eb6be0b/in-southeast-asia-an-exotic-sago-dish-awaits-adventurous-foodies|title=In Southeast Asia, an Exotic Sago Dish Awaits Adventurous Foodies|last=Rodrigues|first=Beverly|work=Explore AirAsia|date=21 November 2022|access-date=10 April 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20250410034640/https://explore.airasia.com/assets/bltb477c7bb4eb6be0b/in-southeast-asia-an-exotic-sago-dish-awaits-adventurous-foodies|archive-date=10 April 2025|url-status=dead}}</ref> In the past, it was a dietary staple for the indigenous people of the region.<ref name="Ambuyat details"/>

In parts of [[Eastern Indonesia]], there is a similar dish called [[Papeda (food)|papeda]], which is usually served with [[stir-fried water spinach]] (''kangkung''), [[papaya]] flower buds, and a [[turmeric]]-tinged fish curry called ''kuah ikan kuning'', flavoured with green chillies, [[lemongrass]], and [[Bay leaf|bay leaves]].<ref name="Asian Foodies AirAsia"/>
