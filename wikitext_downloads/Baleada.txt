A '''baleada''' ({{IPA|es|baleˈaða}}) is a traditional Honduran dish, believed to have originated on the northern coast of the country.{{cn|date=July 2022}} It is composed of a [[flour]] [[tortilla]], filled with a smear of mashed [[Refried beans|refried red beans]] (a variety of bean native to Central and South America), ''[[crema (dairy product)|crema]]'' (''mantequilla blanca''), and crumbled ''queso duro'' (salty hard cheese). This is usually called ''baleada sencilla'' (simple baleada).<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-09-11 |title=Honduran Baleadas - One Happy Housewife |url=https://www.onehappyhousewife.com/honduran-baleadas/ |access-date=2024-03-03 |website=www.onehappyhousewife.com |language=en-US}}</ref> Other ingredients may include [[scrambled eggs]], [[Avocado|avocados]], meat, or plantains.
