'''Salmon chowder''' ({{Langx|fi|lohikeitto}} {{IPA|fi|ˈlohiˌkei̯tːo|}}, {{Langx|sv|laxsoppa}}) is a common dish in [[Finland]] and other [[Nordic countries]]. It consists of [[Salmon as food|salmon]] fillets, boiled [[potato]]es, carrots and [[leek]]s.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Ojakangas |first=Beatrice A. |title=Scandinavian Feasts: Celebrating Traditions Throughout the Year |date=1992 |publisher=[[University of Minnesota Press]] |pages=220 |author-link=Beatrice Ojakangas}}</ref><ref name="David" /> The dish is served hot, and typically seasoned with fresh [[dill]], [[allspice]], salt and black pepper. The soup is made with cream or whole milk, making it a [[chowder]] with a creamy flavour and texture.<ref name="David">{{Cite book |last=Davidson |first=Alan |title=North Atlantic Seafood: A Comprehensive Guide with Recipes |date=1979 |publisher=[[Ten Speed Press]] |pages=360 |author-link=Alan Davidson (food writer)}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Moran |first=Sue |date=April 16, 2022 |title=Finnish Salmon Soup (Lohikeitto) • authentic recipe! |url=https://theviewfromgreatisland.com/finnish-salmon-soup-lohikeitto-recipe/ |access-date=June 18, 2023 |website=The View from Great Island}}</ref>
