'''Tavë kosi''' ("[[soured milk]] [[casserole]]") is a [[national dish]] in [[Albanian cuisine|Albania]]. It is a dish of [[Lamb and mutton|lamb]] (occasionally [[chicken meat|chicken]], then called ''tavë kosi me mish pule'') and [[Rice#Cooking|rice]] baked with a mixture of [[yogurt]] and [[Egg (food)|egg]]s (replacing the original soured milk) added to a [[roux]] ([[wheat flour]] and [[butter]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/food/recipes/albanian_baked_lamb_with_92485|title=Albanian baked lamb with rice (Tavë kosi)|publisher=[[BBC Food]]}}</ref> It is typically seasoned with salt, [[Black pepper|pepper]], [[garlic]] and [[oregano]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2017-02-12 |title=Albanian Tavë Kosi {{!}} Albanian Tavë Elbasani |url=https://www.myalbanianfood.com/recipe/albanian-tave-kosi/ |access-date=2023-10-11 |website=My Albanian Food |language=en-GB}}</ref> The dish is also popular in [[Greece]], [[North Macedonia]], [[Kosovo]] and [[Turkey]], where it is known as ''Elbasan tava'' ({{langx|sq|Tavë Elbasani}} or ''Tava e Elbasanit''), named after the Albanian city of [[Elbasan]].
