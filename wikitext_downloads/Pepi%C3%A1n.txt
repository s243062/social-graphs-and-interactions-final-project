'''Pepián''' is a thick meat stew from Guatemala. It is one of the oldest and most recognizable dishes of [[Guatemalan cuisine]] having as its origin the influences from Guatemala's [[Spanish conquest of Guatemala|colonial past]] and the [[Indigenous cuisine of the Americas|indigenous cuisine]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/cities/2015/feb/24/real-street-food-pepian-from-guatemala |title=Real street food: Pepián from Guatemala |work=The Guardian}}</ref> The meat-based stew, thickened with gourd seeds,<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=RolEyk_23WIC&dq=Pepitoria+Guatemala&pg=PA215|title=Tropical Tree Seed Manual|year=2002 |publisher=University of Minnesota|editor-last=Vozzo|editor-first=J.A.|pages=215–216}}</ref> can be made with beef or chicken (beef is more common in urban centers).<ref>{{cite book|last=Shea|first=Maureen E.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=y6_vkE3Z7XYC&pg=PA61|title=Culture and Customs of Guatemala|year=2001 |publisher=Greenwood Press|pages=61|isbn=9780313305962 }}</ref> Traditional recipes include [[tomatillo]], [[tomato]] and hot chili. Guatemalan restaurants in the United States usually consider the dish to be of [[Ancient Maya cuisine|Maya]] origin.<ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=F4YyDwAAQBAJ&dq=pepian+stew&pg=PT147 |title=The Chicago Food Encyclopedia |date=16 August 2017 |publisher=University of Illinois Press|isbn=9780252099779 }}</ref> It is a popular street food in Guatemalan cities.<ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=BC5pDwAAQBAJ&dq=Pepitoria+pepian+Guatemala&pg=PT953 |title=Lonely Planet's Ultimate Eatlist |date=August 2018 |publisher=Lonely Planet Food|isbn=9781787019768 }}</ref>

In Guatemala, it is considered a [[national dish]].<ref>{{cite book|last1=Sheehan|first1=Sean|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hdJoDwAAQBAJ&pg=PA128|title=Guatemala|last2=Koh|first2=Magdalene|last3=Tess|first3=Alex|date=15 April 2018 |publisher=Cavendish|pages=128|isbn=9781502636256 }}</ref> ''[[Pipián (sauce)|Pipián]]'' is a similar dish from [[Mexican cuisine]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/recipes/red-pepian/12274/ |title=Red Pepian |newspaper=Washington Post}}</ref>
