'''Riz gras''' or '''riz au gras''' is a rice-based dish in [[Beninese cuisine|Beninese]], [[Burkinabé cuisine|Burkinabé]], [[Guinean cuisine|Guinean]], [[Ivorian cuisine|Ivorian]], and [[Togolese cuisine]]s, in [[West African cuisine|West Africa]].<ref name="Albala 2011"/><ref name="Auzias Labourdette 2013"/> It is also prepared in other African countries, such as Senegal, where it is called ''[[tiebou djen]]'' and is prepared with significant amounts of fish and meat.<ref name="Trillo 2008"/><ref name="Albala 2011"/> This dish can be considered a variation of what is known as [[jollof rice]] in [[English-speaking world|English-speaking]] West Africa.

Riz gras is often served at parties in urban areas of Burkina Faso.<ref name="Albala 2011"/> Riz gras is prepared with significant amounts of meat and vegetables, and is usually served with the meat and chunks of vegetables presented atop the rice.<ref name="Albala 2011"/> Additional ingredients used include tomatoes, eggplant, bell peppers, carrots, cabbage, onion, garlic, meat or vegetable [[stock (food)|stock]], oil and salt.<ref name="Albala 2011"/>
